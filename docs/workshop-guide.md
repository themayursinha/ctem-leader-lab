# CTEM Workshop Guide

Use this guide to run a first CTEM planning workshop with security, IT, engineering, GRC, and business leaders.

## Workshop Goals

- Agree on the first crown-jewel services in scope.
- Normalize exposure types beyond CVEs.
- Choose CTEM decision outcomes and escalation rules.
- Define evidence thresholds for validation.
- Create a 30/60/90-day mobilization plan.

## Agenda

| Time | Topic | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 15 min | CTEM shift | Shared language and goal |
| 30 min | Crown-jewel scoping | Services, owners, risk appetite |
| 30 min | Exposure inventory | Data sources and taxonomy |
| 30 min | Prioritization model | Act, Attend, Monitor, Track definitions |
| 30 min | Validation | Safe evidence methods and thresholds |
| 30 min | Mobilization | RACI, SLAs, retest, exception process |

## Facilitation Prompts

- Which service would create board-level concern if compromised or unavailable?
- Which identities or control planes can cross service boundaries?
- Which exposures are repeatedly patched but never validated?
- Which remediation teams need context earlier?
- What evidence is enough to escalate an exposure to Act?
- What exceptions require business owner approval?

## 30/60/90 Plan

### 30 Days

- Run crown-jewel workshop.
- Publish exposure taxonomy.
- Agree on decision outcomes.
- Select first CTEM sprint scope.

### 60 Days

- Normalize scanner, cloud, identity, and secret findings.
- Validate top exposure paths safely.
- Attach evidence packs to remediation work.
- Establish exception and risk acceptance workflow.

### 90 Days

- Report validated exposure reduction.
- Review SLA performance.
- Retest remediated paths.
- Expand scope to the next business service.
