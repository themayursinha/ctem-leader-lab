# CTEM Operating Model

CTEM Leader Lab teaches Continuous Threat Exposure Management as an operating model, not as a scanner category.

## Core Shift

Traditional vulnerability management often starts with a technical backlog and sorts by severity.

CTEM starts with business services, attacker paths, validation evidence, and accountable mobilization.

## Five Stages

| Stage | Goal | Leadership output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Scoping | Define what matters most | Crown-jewel services, owners, risk appetite |
| Discovery | Normalize exposure data | Cross-domain exposure inventory |
| Prioritization | Decide what deserves action | Act, Attend, Monitor, Track outcomes |
| Validation | Prove or disprove risk | Evidence pack and attack-path confidence |
| Mobilization | Reduce exposure | Owner, SLA, retest, exception, escalation |

## Program Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| CISO | Owns CTEM outcomes, risk appetite, and exception policy |
| Business owner | Confirms impact, service criticality, and remediation tradeoffs |
| Security engineering | Owns scoring, validation, exposure quality, and tooling |
| Resolver team | Owns remediation delivery and retest readiness |
| GRC | Tracks risk acceptance, control evidence, and reporting |

## Success Metrics

- Validated critical-path exposure reduction.
- Time from Act decision to owner acceptance.
- SLA compliance for Act and Attend decisions.
- Reopened or recurring exposure rate.
- Percentage of crown-jewel services with current scope and owner.
- Exceptions with business owner and expiry date.
